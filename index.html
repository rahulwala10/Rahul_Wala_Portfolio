The quiet logic that holds buildings up.

My work spans foundations, steel frames, structural assessment, and the safe reuse of existing structures. Selected work

I work on the parts of a building you never see, the foundations, frames and load paths, and the judgement that keeps them safe, buildable and built to last.

I'm a Graduate Structural Engineer based in the UK with experience across India, Northern Ireland and England. My work has spanned structural design, assessment and project delivery across steel, concrete and masonry structures, from ground investigations and foundations through to building superstructures and steel frames.

Alongside my professional role, I'm an active member of the Institution of Structural Engineers Digital Panel and serve as Technical Coordinator for the London Young Members Group.

● Selected focus / Digital & AI IStructE Digital Panel · 2024 to present

An ethical framework for AI in structural engineering.

I researched and authored a technical article examining the ethical risks and challenges of adopting Artificial Intelligence in structural engineering. It explores accountability, transparency, bias in AI-driven decision-making, engineering judgement, data quality and professional responsibility, making the case for ethical governance and human oversight in engineering practice.

The article was published in the October edition of The Structural Engineer, the IStructE's technical magazine, and was subsequently presented at the IStructE AI Conference in Glasgow. The work received the Murray Buxton Award at the IStructE People & Papers Awards in London, recognising its contribution to the responsible integration of AI within the profession.

About

[ 05 ]
Rahul WalaLeicester, UK

An engineer who likes the why as much as the answer.

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of structural engineering, digital technology and practical project delivery. Whether reviewing an existing structure, developing a design option, or coordinating with architects, contractors and other disciplines, I enjoy turning complex technical challenges into clear and buildable solutions.

Much of my work has involved existing buildings and real-world constraints, where engineering judgement matters as much as calculations. I find these projects especially rewarding because they rarely have a single correct answer; they require balancing safety, practicality, cost, programme and sustainability.

Outside project work, I contribute to the profession through technical committees, industry events, mentoring and technical writing. I enjoy sharing knowledge, learning from experienced engineers, and exploring how emerging technologies can support better engineering decisions without replacing the fundamentals the profession is built upon.

When I'm not engineering, you'll usually find me learning something new, exploring ideas around technology and productivity, or working on projects that help engineers and young professionals develop their skills and careers.
